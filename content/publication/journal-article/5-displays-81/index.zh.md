abstract: Smooth pursuit interaction is widely used in eye-controlled systems, yet design guidance for target size and tracking speed under different trajectory shapes remains limited. This study investigates smooth pursuit with the Euclidean algorithm in an eye-control system, examining circular and square trajectories through ergonomic experiments and subjective evaluation. We provide design recommendations for target size and tracking speed to improve interaction performance and user experience in gaze-based interfaces.
